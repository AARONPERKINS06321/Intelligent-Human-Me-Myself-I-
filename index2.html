About the Framework

Parental alienation (PA) is a complex and deeply damaging phenomenon occurring within the context of high-conflict divorce or separation. It describes a situation where children are encouraged by one parent (the favored parent) to unjustly reject the other parent (the targeted parent). This rejection is not based on the child's actual experiences but rather on the emotional manipulation exerted by the favored parent. The specific actions and behaviors utilized by the favored parent to achieve this outcome are termed parental alienation strategies.

This analysis is based on Dr. Amy J. Baker's extensive research, which has identified seventeen primary parental alienation strategies. It is imperative to distinguish parental alienation from legitimate estrangement, where a child's rejection is based on valid reasons such as substantiated abuse or neglect. A definitive identification of PA requires a comprehensive, multi-faceted assessment by a qualified professional, typically using Dr. Baker's five-factor model: (1) rejection of the parent, (2) a prior positive relationship, (3) absence of abuse by the rejected parent, (4) use of alienation strategies by the favored parent, and (5) exhibition of alienation behaviors by the child.

This interactive report focuses primarily on documenting Factor 4—the behaviors of Ms. Candace Perkins as documented in OurFamilyWizard communications—against Dr. Baker's 17 strategies. It serves as a tool to visualize and explore the evidence presented in the case analysis.
